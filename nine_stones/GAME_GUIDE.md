## Introduction

### What is Navakankari?

Navakankari (नवकांकरी), also known as Nine Men's Morris, is an ancient strategy board game for two players. The game has been played for thousands of years across many cultures, with evidence of it being played in ancient Rome, Egypt, and India.

### Game Objective

**Win by achieving one of these conditions:**
1. Reduce your opponent to only 2 pieces on the board
2. Block all of your opponent's pieces so they cannot move

### Game Components

- **Board**: 24 positions arranged in 3 concentric squares connected by lines
- **Pieces**: 
  - Player 1: 9 Saffron pieces 🟠
  - Player 2: 9 Green pieces 🟢
- **Mills**: Any three pieces in a row (horizontal or vertical line)

## How to Play

### Turn Structure

Each turn consists of one of these actions:

1. **Place** a piece (during placing phase)
2. **Move** a piece (during moving phase)
3. **Fly** a piece (during flying phase)
4. **Remove** an opponent's piece (after forming a mill)

### Basic Rules

#### 1. Placing Phase (Turns 1-18)
- Players alternate placing one piece per turn
- Pieces can be placed on any empty position
- This phase ends when all 18 pieces are on the board

#### 2. Moving Phase (After placing)
- Players alternate moving one piece per turn
- Pieces move to adjacent empty positions along the lines
- Adjacent positions are directly connected by a line

#### 3. Flying Phase (When down to 3 pieces)
- When a player has only 3 pieces left, they can "fly"
- Flying allows moving to ANY empty position (not just adjacent)
- This is a significant advantage for the player with 3 pieces

### Forming Mills

**What is a Mill?**
A mill is three of your pieces in a row along any line on the board.

**When you form a mill:**
1. Immediately remove one opponent's piece
2. You cannot remove a piece that's part of a mill (unless ALL opponent pieces are in mills)
3. Choose strategically which piece to remove

**Possible Mills:**
- 16 total mill combinations
- 8 from the outer square
- 4 from the middle square
- 4 from the inner square

### All Possible Mills

**Horizontal Mills:**
```
Outer:   0-1-2, 6-5-4

```

**Vertical Mills:**
```
Outer:   0-7-6, 2-3-4
Middle:  8-15-14, 10-11-12
Inner:   16-23-22, 18-19-20
Across:  1-9-17, 5-13-21
```

## Game Phases

### Phase 1: Placing (Turns 1-18)

**Objective:** Place pieces to form mills and control key positions

**Key Strategies:**
- Place on intersection points (positions where multiple mill lines cross)
- Try to form mills early
- Block opponent's potential mills
- Control the center positions (9, 13, 17, 21)

**Best Positions:**
1. **Center of edges** (1, 3, 5, 7): Part of 4 potential mills
2. **Middle ring corners** (8, 10, 12, 14): Part of 3 potential mills
3. **Connections** (9, 11, 13, 15, 17, 19, 21, 23): Link multiple squares

### Phase 2: Moving (After all pieces placed)

**Objective:** Maneuver pieces to form mills and block opponent

**Key Strategies:**
- Create "double mill" situations (explained below)
- Restrict opponent's movement
- Maintain piece flexibility
- Protect your mills from being broken

**Valid Moves:**
- Only to adjacent empty positions
- Along the lines connecting positions
- One piece per turn

### Phase 3: Flying (When down to 3 pieces)

**Objective:** Exploit flying ability to form unexpected mills

**Advantages:**
- Can move to any empty position
- Much harder for opponent to block
- Can quickly form mills anywhere

**Disadvantages:**
- You're one piece away from losing
- Opponent likely has more pieces
- Need to play defensively too

## Strategy & Tactics

### Opening Strategy

**First Move Recommendations:**
1. **Center positions** (1, 9, 17) - Maximum flexibility
2. **Corners of middle square** (8, 10, 12, 14) - Good mill potential
3. **Avoid corners initially** (0, 2, 4, 6) - Less flexible

**Common Opening Patterns:**
- **Center Control**: Place pieces at 1, 9, 17 to dominate the center
- **Corner Pressure**: Start at 8 and 10 to control the top
- **Balanced Approach**: Mix positions across all three squares

### Mid-Game Tactics

#### 1. Double Mill (Most Powerful)
Create two potential mills that share one piece.

**Example:**
```
If you have pieces at 1-9 and 9-17:
- Move the piece at 9
- Opponent blocks one mill
- You complete the other mill
- Repeat to capture multiple pieces
```

#### 2. Mobility Advantage
- Keep your pieces spread out
- Maintain multiple movement options
- Force opponent into rigid positions

#### 3. Zugzwang
Force opponent into a position where any move worsens their situation.

#### 4. Sacrifice Strategy
- Intentionally lose a piece to gain better position
- Trade pieces when you have a positional advantage

### End-Game Strategy

**When opponent has 3 pieces (flying):**
- Block as many positions as possible
- Form defensive mills
- Control key intersection points
- Force opponent to break their own mills

**When you have 3 pieces (flying):**
- Use flying to surprise opponent
- Form mills from unexpected angles
- Break and reform mills quickly
- Target isolated opponent pieces

## Tips for Beginners

### Do's ✅

1. **Control the Center**
   - Positions 9, 13, 17, 21 are most versatile
   - They connect to multiple potential mills

2. **Think Two Moves Ahead**
   - Where will your piece go next?
   - How will opponent respond?

3. **Watch for Opponent's Mills**
   - Block potential mills early
   - Don't let opponent form double mills

4. **Use the Hint Button**
   - Learn from AI suggestions
   - Understand why certain moves are good

5. **Practice Against AI**
   - Start with Easy difficulty
   - Graduate to Medium, then Hard

### Don'ts ❌

1. **Don't Rush Placement**
   - Take time to plan position
   - Each placement matters

2. **Don't Ignore Defense**
   - Blocking opponent is as important as forming mills
   - Balance offense and defense

3. **Don't Break Your Own Mills**
   - Unless strategically necessary
   - Especially in placing phase

4. **Don't Cluster Pieces**
   - Spread across the board
   - Maintain flexibility

5. **Don't Focus Only on Mills**
   - Position and mobility matter too
   - Think about long-term strategy

## Advanced Techniques

### 1. Mill Building Sequences

**Sequential Mill Formation:**
```
Turn 1: Place at 1
Turn 2: Place at 9
Turn 3: Place at 17 → MILL! Remove opponent piece
Turn 4: Move 9 to elsewhere
Turn 5: Move back to 9 → MILL again!
```

### 2. Positional Sacrifice

Give up a piece to:
- Gain a double mill setup
- Force opponent into a corner
- Create mobility advantage
- Break opponent's formation

### 3. Tempo Control

**Forcing moves:**
- Make moves that require specific responses
- Limit opponent's choices
- Maintain initiative

### 4. Pattern Recognition

**Learn these patterns:**
- Common mill formations
- Dead-end positions (traps)
- Winning configurations
- Defensive formations

### 5. Piece Efficiency

With 3-5 pieces remaining:
- Each piece should serve multiple purposes
- Position for both offense and defense
- Maximize mill-forming potential

## Common Mistakes

### Beginner Mistakes

1. **Creating Half-Mills Too Early**
   - Problem: Placing two pieces in a line too soon
   - Solution: Spread pieces first, form mills strategically

2. **Ignoring the Outer Square**
   - Problem: Focusing only on inner positions
   - Solution: Use all three squares equally

3. **Breaking Mills Unnecessarily**
   - Problem: Moving pieces out of formed mills
   - Solution: Only break mills to form better ones

4. **Not Using Removals Wisely**
   - Problem: Removing pieces randomly
   - Solution: Remove opponent's key pieces or potential mill pieces

### Intermediate Mistakes

1. **Allowing Double Mills**
   - Problem: Not recognizing when opponent can form repeating mills
   - Solution: Block the pivot position

2. **Poor Piece Trading**
   - Problem: Trading pieces when in a worse position
   - Solution: Trade only when ahead or to gain advantage

3. **Neglecting the Flying Phase**
   - Problem: Not planning for when opponent reaches 3 pieces
   - Solution: Control key positions before opponent flies

## FAQ

### Q: Can I remove a piece from an opponent's mill?
**A:** No, unless ALL of the opponent's pieces are in mills. Then you can remove any piece.

### Q: Can I form the same mill twice?
**A:** Yes! You can break a mill and reform it to remove another piece.

### Q: What happens if I can't move?
**A:** You lose the game. This is one of the win conditions.

### Q: Can I move through other pieces?
**A:** No. You can only move to empty adjacent positions.

### Q: When can I start flying?
**A:** When you have exactly 3 pieces remaining on the board.

### Q: Can I undo a move?
**A:** Yes, click the "Undo" button. You can undo up to 20 moves.

### Q: How does the hint system work?
**A:** The AI analyzes the board and suggests a good move. Use it to learn!

### Q: What's the best difficulty for beginners?
**A:** Start with Easy, then try Medium after winning consistently.

### Q: Can I win in the placing phase?
**A:** No, you can only win during the moving phase when opponent has <3 pieces or can't move.

### Q: Is there a time limit?
**A:** No, take as much time as you need to think.
